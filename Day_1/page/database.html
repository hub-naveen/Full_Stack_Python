Storing and Retrieving Data

Databases are the foundation of any application that needs to persist data. They are specialized systems for storing, managing, and retrieving information efficiently.

Types of Databases

  • Relational (SQL): Structured data in tables (e.g., MySQL, PostgreSQL).
  • Non-Relational (NoSQL): Flexible document or key-value stores (e.g., MongoDB, Redis).
  • Graph Databases: Specialized for complex relationships (e.g., Neo4j).
